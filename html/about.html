Health care in Egypt

In Egypt there are healthcare services from the public and private sectors. Public health coverage is provided through the Ministry of Health, which operates a series of medical facilities that provide free health services. There are two types of private insurance companies. The General Authority for Health Insurance covers working people, students, and widows through premiums deducted from employees' salaries and employers' schedules. It operates its own network of medical facilities and sometimes contracts with private healthcare providers. Therapeutic care organization works for select government organizations and contracts with other entities to provide care. Also private insurance options, and a network of private healthcare providers and medical facilities. In addition, many mosques run their own clinics, especially in large cities. Many churches offer subsidized or free clinics.

The medical care provided by the public health insurance system is usually of poor quality. Government hospitals are known to be neglectful and generally provide minimal care. Only about 6% of Egyptians covered by the Health Insurance Authority actually use its services due to dissatisfaction with the level of services it funds. In 2008/2009, 72% of health expenditures in Egypt were paid out of pocket for people seeking treatment.

Egypt is currently working on reforming its public healthcare system to improve its quality. A bill was approved in October 2017 that would increase the national contribution from 4% of current wages to 5%, with 4% to be paid by employers and 1% by employees. The Egyptian Medical Syndicate is concerned that this could lead to the privatization of public hospitals, especially those that may not meet the quality standards stipulated in the new law. They say only 20% of the 660 government hospitals are committed to safety and infection control standards.

Workforce

In 2014, about 70,000 human doctors worked in the Ministry's hospitals, and the number of pharmacists is estimated at 20,000, and the number of veterinarians is about 15,000. It rose to 91,000 human doctors, and about 22,000 dentists and 196,000 nurses in 2019. Doctors' salaries in Egypt are low. Monthly salaries range between 1,218 EGP ($69) and 6,365 EGP ($361) per month. Among the members of the Egyptian Medical Syndicate, only about a third work in the country.

Pharmaceutical production

In the latest report of the Arab Federation of Producers of Medicines and Medical Supplies of the League of Arab States in 2011, Egypt ranked first in the Arab world in the production of medicines, with a production value of $2810 million. It also ranked first in terms of the number of pharmaceutical factories, amounting to 85 factories.

Infrastructure and hospitals of Egypt

Hospitals in the government sector contain about 95,000 beds, about 9,500 intensive care units, more than half of which are in university hospitals. The number of public hospitals is more than 660, followed by health offices and health care units, which are smaller facilities than hospitals, with 5,314 facilities nationwide. About the governorate

North Sinai Governorate is located in the northeast of Egypt between longitudes 32.34 east and latitudes 29.31 north. It is bordered to the north by the Mediterranean Sea at a length of 220 km. To the south, a line extends from the south of the Mitla corridor to Ras al-Naqab. It is bordered to the east by the political border of Egypt with the Gaza Strip. As for Israel, to the west, it represents a line extending from the Metla Corridor in the south to Baluza in the north. Sinai is of great strategic importance (especially North Sinai), as it represents the eastern fortress of Egypt, and it is the passage through which most of the invasions that targeted Egypt crossed, whether in ancient or modern history.

The area of North Sinai is about 27,564 square kilometers, and its estimated population is 419,200 people for the year 2013. 86.5% of the population lives on the coastal strip, and the percentage of the coastal centers is 21.4% of the total area of the governorate. The geographical features in North Sinai are divided into two distinct types, the first of which is the coastal environment, which includes plains. The northern region that borders the Mediterranean Sea at a depth of 20-40 km and is covered with undulating and flat sand dunes.

As for the second type of geographical features, it is the desert environment that prevails in the middle of North Sinai, which is mostly located in the plateau region, which is characterized by the presence of a group of high and separate mountains such as Mount Al-Maghara (776 meters above sea level), Jabal Al-Halal (881 meters above sea level) and Jabal Yalaq (1094 meters above sea level).
