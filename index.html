What are health services?

Health services are defined as the care he receives; patients, families, communities, and populations, by; Medical professionals, health care personnel, and health care institutions. Health services include; Emergency, preventive, rehabilitative, home care, long-term services, hospital and diagnostic care, etc.

Basically, health services aim to enable all patients to have access to accessible and high-quality health care. Therefore, successful health services require many types of health services in addition to the availability of effective service providers.

Healthcare levels

There are four levels through which the stages of health care pass, and these levels are usually described as a hierarchical structure that follows depending on the complexity and severity of the health challenges being addressed and the nature of the relationship between the patient and the service provider. The following are the levels of health care :

Primary level of care

It is the level concerned with providing health services to people rather than focusing on the diseases themselves, it is the first stage of health care that people receive when they have medical concerns or needs, and it is the level that people use the most.

Second level of care

It is the level that specializes in providing health care to patients who have been transferred from the primary care level to obtain specific and specialized care, and this level of care is usually provided in hospitals and clinics, and therefore the secondary level of care is concerned with treating the most serious cases that need support from a specialist.

Third level of care

It is the level that provides highly specialized health care, which usually provides care over a long period of time, as this level ensures that the patient receives advanced and complex diagnoses, procedures and treatments provided by medical specialists in the most modern conditions.

Fourth level of care

The fourth level of care is an extension of the third level, where the fourth level provides more specialized health care, which is usually provided in a very limited number of national or international centers, as this level includes experimental medicine and some types of diagnostic or surgical procedures that are not common.

Health services facilities

Health services facilities are not limited to hospitals and clinics only, but also include many other facilities. Some of these facilities are as follows :

Ambulatory surgery centers

It is a facility that offers surgical procedures outside the hospital environment at a lower cost than hospitals with reduced risk of infection.

Birthing centres

They are centers specialized in childbirth operations, as these facilities provide an environment for childbirth in which the mother feels more comfortable, but these facilities are not equipped except for cases of natural childbirth.

Blood banks

These are facilities where blood and platelets can be donated, stored and sorted for use by patients who need it.

Diabetes education centers

They are centers specialized in providing assistance to diabetics to manage their disease, and help people at risk of developing diabetes to avoid it.

Dialysis centers

They are facilities that help kidney patients through regular dialysis, as these patients need dialysis three times a week.

Ambulance components

The ambulance in its latest form is an urgent care vehicle, specially designed to provide a cabin for the driver and a cabin for the patient, preferably separate from the first, and capable of accommodating two ambulance officers and two patients on two stretchers in a way that enables at least one patient to be given intensive care during transportation.

Such a vehicle must carry equipment and materials to enable urgent care, at the scene of the accident and during transportation, to protect workers and patients in perilous conditions, to carry out normal rescue operations, to ensure the maximum possible protection and comfort and to avoid deterioration of the patient's condition or loss of life.

The credit for inventing ambulances, in fact, goes back to Dominique Jean, chief surgeon of Napoleon's army, as he was keen to prepare operating rooms and bring them with him to the battlefield to treat high-ranking officers, and ambulances did not spread in the United States until after World War II, although From the presence of horse-drawn ambulances since the late 1800 AD.

Types of ambulances

There are several types of fully equipped ambulances, which differ according to the vehicle or means of transport, namely :

  • Vans (by far the most common type).
  • Four-wheel drive vehicles.
  • Motor cycles.
  • Buses.
  • Planes.
  • Helicopters.

Paramedics job inside ambulances

A paramedic is a trained healthcare professional who is in an ambulance to provide emergency medical care, caring for people with :

  • Heart attacks.
  • strokes.
  • Spinal injuries.
  • Cases of overuse of medications, and overdose of them.
  • Mental illness.
